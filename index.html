No-Equipment Glute Workout Guide

1. Banded Hip Thrust

â–¼

Primary Muscles

Gluteus Maximus (main part of the butt)

Equipment

  • Loop resistance bands
  • Sturdy bench/sofa/chair

Setup

  • Sit on floor with upper back against bench/sofa edge
  • Place resistance band across hips and secure under your shoulders or anchor to something sturdy
  • Plant feet flat, shoulder-width apart
  • Optional: Place second loop band above knees for more glute activation

Execution

Tuck chin, brace core
Drive through heels to lift hips up against band resistance
Lift until body forms straight line from shoulders to knees
Squeeze glutes hard at top for 1-2 seconds
Lower hips down under control
Repeat. Exhale up, inhale down

Key Form Cues

DO

  • Drive through heels
  • Keep chin tucked
  • Squeeze glutes HARD at top
  • Control descent
  • Keep knees tracking over feet

DON'T

  • Push through toes
  • Arch lower back
  • Look up at ceiling
  • Let knees cave inward

Feel It

Intense contraction in glutes at top. If using knee band, also in outer glutes.

2. Banded Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

â–¼

Primary Muscles

Hamstrings and Glutes

Equipment

  • Loop resistance band

Setup

  • Stand with feet hip-width apart on middle of resistance band
  • Hold the ends of the band in each hand
  • Stand tall, chest up, shoulders back
  • Slight bend in knees - maintain this bend throughout

Execution

Push hips back (hinge, don't squat)
Let hands lower straight down, keeping tension on the band
Keep back flat the entire time
Lower until you feel hamstring stretch
Drive hips forward to stand up against band resistance
Squeeze glutes at the top
Repeat. Inhale down, exhale up

Key Form Cues

DO

  • Hinge at hips
  • Keep back flat
  • Keep hands close to legs
  • Feel hamstring stretch
  • Drive hips forward
  • Maintain soft knees

DON'T

  • Round lower back
  • Let hands drift forward
  • Squat by bending knees more
  • Lose tension in the band

Feel It

Hamstring stretch going down, strong hamstrings and glutes coming up

3. Bodyweight Bulgarian Split Squat

â–¼

Primary Muscles

Glutes and Quads (front leg), Glute Medius (stability)

Equipment

  • Sturdy chair/bench/sofa
  • Optional: Loop resistance band

Setup

  • Stand 2-3 feet in front of chair, facing away
  • Place top of one foot on chair edge
  • Position front foot for knee over ankle when lowering
  • Optional: Step on middle of band with front foot and hold ends at shoulders

Execution

Lower body straight down by bending front knee
Descend until front thigh is roughly parallel to floor
Keep torso stable, core tight
Drive through front foot to stand up
Complete all reps on one leg before switching

Key Form Cues

DO

  • Lower straight down
  • Keep front knee tracking over foot
  • Drive through front foot
  • Keep core braced
  • Slight forward lean for more glutes

DON'T

  • Let knee collapse inward
  • Bounce at bottom
  • Lean excessively
  • Push off back foot too much

Feel It

Strong work in quad and glute of front leg, side glute for stability

4. Banded Hip Abduction (Standing)

â–¼

Primary Muscles

Gluteus Medius and Minimus (Side/Upper Glutes)

Equipment

  • Loop resistance band

Setup

  • Place band around legs: ankles (harder) or above knees (easier)
  • Stand feet hip-width apart with slight band tension
  • Slight athletic stance with soft knees
  • Optional: Hold wall/chair for balance

Execution

Shift weight onto one leg (keep knee slightly bent)
Lift other leg out to side, leading with heel
Keep torso upright (no leaning sideways)
Squeeze outer/upper glute of moving leg
Control leg back, maintaining band tension
Complete all reps on one side before switching

Key Form Cues

DO

  • Keep torso upright
  • Control movement out and in
  • Lead with heel
  • Squeeze side/upper glute
  • Keep core braced

DON'T

  • Lean torso sideways
  • Swing the leg
  • Point toes up
  • Let band snap leg back

Feel It

Burn in side/upper glutes of moving leg, standing leg glute works for stability

Remember

Start with lighter resistance bands that allow perfect form. Focus on mind-muscle connection. Consistently apply progressive overload by gradually increasing band strength or reps/sets over time for growth.
